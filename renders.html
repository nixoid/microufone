TPU Inflatable Envelope — Geometry Options

Envelope topologies from §2 and flight modes from §4 of specs. Drag to rotate each view; scroll to zoom.

§2.1 · Flat circles + sidewall band

Two circular faces joined by a rectangular band
weld seam panel edge fiberglass ring wire + tape
  • "Pillow on a barrel" silhouette — visible cylindrical sidewall, not a true lens.
  • Three panels to cut (two circles + a rectangle) instead of two.
  • More total weld length than the bent-rim variant.

§2.2 · Bent rims, thin equatorial collar

Two discs with a ~2 cm bent rim, overlap-welded
rim pleats fiberglass ring wire + tape
  • Near-lens silhouette — the bent rim collapses to a barely-visible equatorial collar.
  • Two panels only; no separate sidewall band to cut, jig, or align.
  • Pleating modest at small w (~12 cm gather per edge for a 2 cm lip on a 3 m disc).
  • Peel-loaded equator accepted — structural margin not critical for festival display.

§2.3 · Gored (orange-peel)

12 meridional gores welded edge-to-edge
gore seam gore panel fiberglass ring wire + tape
  • True curved surface with no forced stretch — smoothest inflated shape.
  • 8–24 gores; total weld length and leak paths scale with gore count.
  • 1–2 mm cut error per gore accumulates across 12+ panels.

§4 · Flight modes

Same §2.2 envelope and fiberglass ring — different orientation and ground anchoring.

§4.1 · Horizontal disc (Saucer)

Ring horizontal; disc faces up and down
fiberglass ring wire + tape ground tether ground anchor
  • 4 ground tethers at ring cardinal points — inverted-pyramid footprint.
  • Restoring moments against tilt; audience views the glowing disc from below.
  • Best for low-to-moderate wind and overhead display contexts.

§4.2 · Vertical disc (Microphone)

Disc perpendicular to ground; 3-tether tripod
fiberglass ring wire + tape ground tether ground anchor
  • 3 tethers at ring mount points (same as envelope wire mounts), omitting the upper one — each pulls in a different direction.
  • Pendulum stability; center of buoyancy above tether attachments.
  • Best for moderate wind and side-on display beside the audience.
